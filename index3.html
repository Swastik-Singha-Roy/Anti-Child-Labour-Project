Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is child labour?
Child labour refers to the employment of children in any work that deprives them of their childhood, interferes with their ability to attend regular school, and is mentally, physically, socially, or morally harmful.
2. What are the main causes of child labour in India?
The main causes of child labour in India include poverty, lack of education, inadequate enforcement of labour laws, high demand for cheap labour, and cultural factors that accept child labour as a norm.
3. What are the key laws governing child labour in India?
The key laws include:
  • The Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986: Prohibits employment of children below 14 years in hazardous occupations and processes.
  • The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 (RTE Act): Ensures education for children between 6-14 years.
  • The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015: Provides for the care, protection, and rehabilitation of child victims.
  • The Factories Act, 1948 and The Mines Act, 1952: Prohibit employment of children below 14 in factories and mines.
4. What age is defined as a child under Indian child labour laws?
Under Indian child labour laws, a child is defined as any person below the age of 14 years.
5. Are there any exceptions to the prohibition of child labour in India?
Yes, the Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2016 allows children below 14 years to work in family businesses and as artists in the entertainment industry, provided it is outside school hours and does not interfere with their education.
6. What penalties exist for violating child labour laws in India?
Employers found violating child labour laws can face imprisonment ranging from 6 months to 2 years and fines between INR 20,000 to INR 50,000. Repeat offenders can face higher penalties.
7. How can the public help combat child labour in India?
The public can help by:
  • Reporting instances of child labour to authorities.
  • Supporting organizations that work to eradicate child labour.
  • Raising awareness about the importance of education and the harmful effects of child labour.
8. What role does the government play in eradicating child labour?
The government enforces laws, conducts inspections, provides education through schemes like the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan, and runs rehabilitation and welfare programs for affected children.
9. What are the rights of children under the Indian Constitution concerning child labour?
The Indian Constitution guarantees:
  • Article 21A: The right to free and compulsory education for children aged 6-14 years.
  • Article 24: Prohibits employment of children in hazardous industries.
  • Article 39(e): Directs the State to protect children from abuse and exploitation.
10. What international conventions has India ratified regarding child labour?
India has ratified key International Labour Organization (ILO) conventions, including:
  • Convention No. 138: Minimum Age Convention.
  • Convention No. 182: Worst Forms of Child Labour Convention.
11. How does child labour affect children?
Child labour adversely affects children by hindering their education, exposing them to physical and mental health risks, and depriving them of a normal childhood.
12. Are there any government schemes specifically aimed at preventing child labour?
Yes, schemes like the National Child Labour Project (NCLP) provide for the rehabilitation of child labourers and mainstreaming them into the formal education system.
